Our Values

Everything we do is guided by six core values. These are not posters on a wall. They shape how we show up for you every day.

Authenticity

We honour who you actually are, not who others think you should be.

Connection

We build genuine relationships, not transactional ones.

Sovereignty

Your life, your choices. We follow your lead.

Joy

Joy is not a bonus. It is part of the plan.

Safety

Feeling safe comes first. Nothing happens without it.

Accessibility

We remove barriers, including the ones inside our own services.

02

Our Commitment to You

We commit to delivering services that are built around who you actually are. This means:

  • We will always treat you with dignity and respect.
  • We will listen to what you want, not just what others think you need.
  • We will not use functioning labels, deficit language, or pathologising frameworks.
  • We will be honest with you, even when it is hard.
  • We will not provide supports we are not qualified or registered to provide.
  • We will keep your information private and secure.
  • We will tell you if something changes that affects your services.
  • We will always have a complaints process that is genuinely accessible to you.

You are not here to fit our model. Our model is here to fit you.

03

Our Services

We are an NDIS registered provider delivering supports across the Sunshine Coast. All of our services are neurodivergent-affirming, attachment-based, and evidence-informed.

Support Coordination

We help you understand and use your NDIS plan. This includes connecting you with providers, navigating the NDIS, building your capacity to manage your own plan, and supporting you through reviews and plan changes. We also offer Specialist Support Coordination and Psychosocial Recovery Coaching.

Early Childhood Supports

For children under 9 and their families. We provide early childhood supports, family coaching, and connection to community. Our approach centres the family as the expert on their child.

Daily Living and Community Supports

We support development of skills for everyday life, and help you take part in the things that matter to you, in the community. This includes participation at The Nest, our sensory-considered space in Caloundra.

Therapeutic and Specialist Supports

We offer counselling and therapeutic supports through our clinical team. Our allied health partnerships extend our capacity to support you across a range of therapeutic needs.

NDIS Registered

Registration No. 4-K6GFD9R. We are registered under support categories 0106, 0117, 0118, 0125, and 0128. All services are claimable under your NDIS plan where the relevant support category is funded.

Getting Started

Starting with a new provider can feel overwhelming. We have tried to make this as straightforward as possible.

1

Book a conversation

No forms, no assessments. Just a conversation to see if we are the right fit. Book through our Calendly link.

2

Complete an Expression of Interest

Before we move to intake, we ask you to complete a short Expression of Interest. This gives us a brief picture of who you are, what you are looking for, and whether we have the right capacity and registration to support you well.

3

Complete your intake form

Once we confirm we are a good fit, we will send you a link to our intake form. This helps us understand what matters to you, your goals, and any important information about your support needs.

4

Sign your Service Agreement

Your Service Agreement sets out what we will provide, how much it costs, and what both parties agree to. You will have time to read it carefully and ask any questions before signing.

5

Begin your supports

Your first sessions are about building connection and understanding your baseline. We go at your pace. There is no rush here.

Who can access our services?

You need an active NDIS plan with funding in the relevant support category. Under the current NDIS framework, funding is based on your assessed support needs. If you are not sure whether your plan covers what you need, your Support Coordinator or Local Area Coordinator can help you check.

Additional documentation

Depending on your personal circumstances, we may need to request additional documentation before or during your intake. This could include things like your NDIS plan, supporting reports, or consent forms from other providers. We will let you know what is needed and why, and we will always try to make this as straightforward as possible.

Support Planning

Your supports are planned around what matters to you, with your goals at the centre. In your first sessions, we will work with you to develop a Support Plan that reflects what matters to you in your own words.

Your Support Plan is a living document. It changes as you change. We review it regularly with you, and you can ask to revisit it at any time.

Goals in your own words

We use Goal Attainment Scaling (GAS) to track your progress. This means you describe each goal in your own language, and we build a scale together that describes what success looks like for you specifically, from baseline through to better than expected outcomes. The language is yours, not ours.

Plan review support

If you receive a notice that your plan is ending or your funding period is changing, contact us straight away. We can help you prepare for your reassessment and make sure nothing falls through the gap.

07

Our Team

Every person on our team is here because they genuinely believe in what we do. Many of our team members are neurodivergent themselves. We do not perform inclusion. We live it.

Who might support you

Depending on your support needs and goals, you may work with practitioners from our clinical and coordination team, therapy assistants, support workers, or allied health partners. Our team includes psychologists, registered support coordinators, early childhood practitioners, art therapists, and allied health professionals.

Many of our team members are neurodivergent themselves. You will always be introduced to the person working with you before sessions begin, and you will be told about their role and qualifications. You have the right to request a different worker at any time, no explanation needed.

08

Code of Conduct

All Neurodivergent Empowered staff are bound by the NDIS Code of Conduct. This means every person who supports you must:

  • Act with respect for your individual rights to freedom of expression, self-determination, and decision-making
  • Respect your privacy
  • Provide supports and services in a safe and competent manner with care and skill
  • Act with integrity, honesty, and transparency
  • Promptly take steps to raise and act on concerns about matters that might affect the quality and safety of supports
  • Take all reasonable steps to prevent and respond to all forms of violence, exploitation, neglect, and abuse
  • Take all reasonable steps to prevent sexual misconduct

If you ever feel that a team member has not upheld this code, please let us know. Section 15 explains how to raise a complaint.

09

What We Ask of You

We keep our asks simple. To get the most from your supports, we ask that you:

  • Let us know as early as possible if you need to cancel or reschedule a session. Our cancellation policy is detailed in your Service Agreement.
  • Tell us when something is not working. We cannot change what we do not know about.
  • Keep us updated on anything that might affect your safety or the safety of others.
  • Let us know if your NDIS plan changes, including funding period changes and plan variations.
  • Treat our staff with respect. We extend the same to you always.
Cancellations

We understand that plans change and that some days are harder than others. Our cancellation policy is in your Service Agreement. For short-notice cancellations, NDIS rules apply regarding what can be claimed. We will always be upfront about this.

10

What You Can Expect from Us

In addition to our commitments in Section 02, here is what you can expect day to day:

  • Clear communication about your sessions, including any changes to staff, location, or timing.
  • A consistent support worker wherever possible, so you do not have to re-explain yourself every week.
  • Session notes written in plain language and shared with you on request.
  • Honest conversations about what is working and what is not.
  • Referrals to other providers when we cannot meet a need ourselves.
  • A safe space to be exactly who you are without masking.

If we ever fall short of this, we want to know. You are not a burden for raising it.

How billing works

Plan managed: ZandaHealth sends an automated invoice to you and your plan manager the day after your session. Your plan manager processes the payment from your NDIS funds. You do not pay anything out of pocket.

Self managed: ZandaHealth sends an automated invoice to you the day after your session. You pay us directly and then claim reimbursement through your NDIS participant portal.

NDIA managed: We submit a payment request directly through the NDIS provider portal after your session. The NDIA pays us from your plan funds. You do not receive an invoice and do not need to do anything.

12

Conflict of Interest

A conflict of interest happens when a personal or business interest could influence the way we provide your supports. We take this seriously, especially in Support Coordination roles where we are helping you choose providers.

We manage conflicts of interest by:

  • Declaring any conflicts in writing and keeping a register
  • Never recommending our own services as a Support Coordinator without disclosing the conflict first
  • Giving you a genuine choice of providers and supporting you to compare options
  • Seeking your signed consent before proceeding if a conflict exists

If you ever feel that a conflict of interest has affected your supports, this can be raised as a complaint. See Section 15 for how to do this.

13

Privacy and Confidentiality

Your privacy is important to us. We collect and store only the information we need to provide your supports safely and effectively.

What we collect

We collect personal information including your name, contact details, NDIS plan information, health and support needs, and records of the supports we provide. We collect this information from you directly, and in some cases from your support network or other providers with your consent.

How we use it

We use your information to plan and deliver your supports, communicate with you and your nominated support network, and meet our NDIS reporting obligations. We do not share your information without your consent except where we are required to by law.

Your rights

  • You can request access to your records at any time.
  • You can ask us to correct information that is wrong.
  • You can withdraw consent for us to share your information, with some exceptions required by law.
  • You can make a privacy complaint through our complaints process (Section 15) or directly to the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner.

Our Privacy and Information Management Policy is available on our website. Your privacy consent form is part of your intake process.

14

Your Safety

Your safety is our first responsibility. We take an active, not reactive, approach to safety.

Safe environment

The Nest has been designed with sensory safety in mind. We monitor and adjust lighting, sound, and the physical environment based on participant feedback and our observations. Overwhelm is not the default here.

Risk assessment

We complete a risk assessment as part of your intake. This is reviewed whenever your circumstances change. You are part of this process, not just the subject of it.

Restrictive practices

We do not use restrictive practices. If a Behaviour Support Plan exists for you, we will discuss this with you and your support network and ensure any practices are authorised and reviewed as required by law.

Reporting obligations

Some incidents must be reported to the NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission. We will always tell you if we are required to make a report, what we are reporting, and why. Section 16 has more information on incident management.

15

Feedback and Complaints

Feedback is how we get better. Complaints are taken seriously and responded to promptly. You will not be treated any differently for raising a concern.

How to give feedback or make a complaint

  • Talk to any team member directly
  • Contact us through ZandaHealth
  • Complete our written Feedback and Complaints Form
  • Submit anonymously by post or through an independent advocate

What happens next

We will acknowledge your complaint within 2 business days. We will investigate and respond within 14 days. Complex complaints may take longer, and we will keep you informed of progress. You can be supported by an advocate throughout this process.

If you are not satisfied

You can raise a complaint with the NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission at any time. You do not have to go through us first.

NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission

Phone: 1800 035 544. Website: ndiscommission.gov.au. Hours: Monday to Friday, 9am to 5pm local time.

16

Incident Management

An incident is any event that has caused or could cause harm to you or others. We take all incidents seriously, including near misses.

Reportable incidents

Some incidents must be reported to the NDIS Commission within set timeframes. These include:

  • Unexpected death of a participant
  • Serious injury of a participant
  • Abuse or neglect of a participant
  • Unlawful sexual or physical contact
  • Use of a restrictive practice not in a participant's Behaviour Support Plan
  • Any event that causes serious harm or risk of serious harm

What we do

We record all incidents. We notify relevant parties as required. We review what happened and put changes in place to prevent it from happening again. We will tell you what we found and what we changed.

17

Emergencies and Disasters

We have plans in place to keep your supports going during emergencies, natural disasters, and other disruptions.

Your emergency plan

As part of your intake, we will work with you to document your personal emergency information, including who to contact and any specific support needs you have during an emergency. This is stored securely in your file and reviewed regularly.

If our services are disrupted

We will contact you as early as possible if a session or service is affected by an emergency or disaster. We will tell you what your options are and how we plan to continue your supports.

In an immediate emergency

Call 000. Do not wait for us.

Sunshine Coast Disaster Hub

For local emergency information during major events, visit disasterhub.sunshinecoast.qld.gov.au or call the Sunshine Coast Council on 07 5475 7272.

18

Leaving Our Service

You can end your services with us at any time. Your Service Agreement sets out the notice period, which is generally 14 days, but we can discuss this with you.

What we will do when you leave

  • Provide a summary of your progress and goals for your records and any incoming provider
  • Complete any outstanding reports or NDIS documentation
  • Return any property and destroy or archive your information in line with legal requirements
  • Support a warm transition to a new provider if you wish

If we end services

We will only end services in limited circumstances, such as if there is a risk to staff safety, or if your needs have changed beyond what we can safely support. We will give you as much notice as possible and support you to find alternative services.

We will always aim to leave the door open. Circumstances change, and if things are different down the track, you are welcome to come back.

19

Can Someone Speak for Me?

Yes. Always. You have the right to have a support person, family member, friend, or independent advocate involved in any aspect of your services.

Independent advocacy

An independent advocate is someone who acts solely in your interests. They are not connected to us. You can involve an independent advocate at any time, including when making a complaint, in planning conversations, or when making decisions about your services.

Advocacy Queensland

Advocacy Queensland provides free, independent advocacy for people with disability. Phone: 07 3844 4200. Website: advocacyqld.org.au

NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission

The Commission also provides information about your rights. Phone: 1800 035 544. Website: ndiscommission.gov.au

Advocate or Support Person Request Form

If you would like to formally nominate someone to act on your behalf, ask us for an Advocate or Support Person Request Form. This authorises them to communicate with us on your behalf.

Privacy Policy

We handle personal information in line with the privacy and dignity expectations in the NDIS Practice Standards and the privacy obligations set out under the Australian Privacy Principles. We only collect information that is relevant to providing supports, meeting legal and compliance requirements, responding to enquiries, and administering our services.

  • Information is collected, used, stored and shared only for support, service, safety, billing, legal, and regulatory purposes.
  • Privacy and confidentiality information should be provided in the language, mode of communication, and terms most likely to be understood by the person receiving supports.
  • You can ask to access or correct your personal information.
  • You can raise a privacy concern or complaint and it will be handled fairly and without reprisal.

If you need privacy information in a different format or communication style, please let us know and we will work with you to provide it in a way that is more accessible.

Accessibility

We want this site to be usable across different communication, sensory and access needs. Practical improvements we can continue to add across the site include:

  • clear keyboard navigation and visible focus states
  • stronger colour contrast where needed
  • plain language and consistent headings
  • alt text for meaningful images
  • captions and transcripts for video or audio content
  • forms with clear labels, instructions and error messages
  • support for reduced motion and less visual overload
  • larger tap targets and readable text at zoom

If something on the site is hard to read, hear, navigate or use, please tell us so we can improve it.
