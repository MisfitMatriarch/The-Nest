Your Goals

This is where we get to know what really matters to you. Each goal gets its own card where you describe it in your own words and tell us what achieving it would look like at different levels. You can add as many goals as you like - even one is a great start.

What matters most to you right now

Noted. We will explore this with you in conversation at your first session. There is no pressure to have this worked out in advance.

Your individual goals

For each goal, tell us what it is and how important it is to you. Then describe what different levels of progress would look like - this is called Goal Attainment Scaling (GAS) and it helps us measure whether what we are doing is actually working for you.

How the scale works: +2 is much better than expected. +1 is better than expected. 0 is roughly what you expected to achieve. -1 is less progress than hoped. -2 is much less progress. You set what those levels mean for you - there are no wrong answers.
Noted. We will work through goal setting together at your first session. You do not need to have goals ready before we start.

Step 8 of 8

Service Agreement

The NDIS Practice Standards require us to offer you a written Service Agreement. You have the right to choose how you want to handle this.

What is a Service Agreement?

A Service Agreement is a written record of what supports we will provide, how often, the costs involved, your rights, and how either of us can make changes or end the arrangement. It is not a lock-in contract. You can change or exit at any time with notice. The NDIS Practice Standards say we must offer this to you in writing.
