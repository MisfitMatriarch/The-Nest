Overview

What We Offer

Neurodivergent Empowered provides interest-based participation supports, support coordination, psychosocial recovery coaching, early childhood supports, capacity building, community participation, and therapeutic supports.

Our services are delivered in a way that respects neurodivergent identity, prioritises participant authority, and adapts to how your nervous system actually works on any given day. We are an NDIS registered provider operating on the Sunshine Coast, Queensland, and we work with participants across a range of registration codes depending on their individual plan and goals.

Individual Programmes

Interest-Based Participation Supports

These are structured supports designed around genuine interest and natural engagement, not programmes built around behavioural compliance. When participation is interest-driven, skill development happens alongside it, rather than as the goal in itself.

Sessions are delivered at The Nest, our purpose-built space at Caloundra Rumba Resort on the Sunshine Coast. The Nest is a sensory-aware, low-demand environment designed to reduce the cost of showing up. It is not a clinical room. It is a place where people can regulate, connect, and participate in ways that make sense for them.

Every session at The Nest is delivered by a practitioner who is a trained therapy assistant or therapist in training, working under clinical supervision. We work exclusively with Autistic and neurodivergent people. This is not a general disability service that accommodates neurodivergence. It is a specialist environment built specifically for it, by people who understand it from the inside.

That distinction matters. Mainstream community groups, hobby clubs, and general disability services do not provide neurodivergent-affirming facilitation. They do not create an Autistic peer environment. They do not include clinical oversight of participation. Research consistently shows that Autistic people mask significantly more in neurotypical social settings, and that sustained masking elevates stress, depletes capacity, and contributes to burnout. The support NE provides is not a more accessible version of what already exists in the community. It addresses a documented need that community settings are not equipped to meet.

Autistic and neurodivergent people face specific disability-related barriers to social participation that most community settings do not address. Unstructured social environments, neurotypical communication norms, unpredictable sensory conditions, and the demand to mask are not inconveniences. They are the reason participation often does not happen at all. Every session at The Nest is designed around removing those specific barriers, not around managing the person who faces them. The interest is the vehicle. The barrier is what we are funded to address.

Sessions connect directly to NDIS plan goals including building friendships and social connection, increasing community participation, developing daily living and communication skills, and building capacity for greater independence. You do not need a specific goal for every session, but the support is always working toward something real in your life.

Interest-matched participation also represents strong value for NDIS funding. Connecting Autistic participants with others who share their interests in a specialist-facilitated environment reduces social isolation, builds peer connection that exists outside funded supports, and develops skills and confidence that reduce reliance on higher-intensity supports over time. We actively look for where shared delivery is appropriate so that costs stay manageable while outcomes stay high.

Every Australian has the opportunity to connect with people who share their interests. For many Autistic people, that opportunity has not been reliably available - not because the interests are not there, but because the environments where those connections happen were not built for them. These sessions exist to change that. Connection through shared interest is not a programme extra. It is what an ordinary life looks like.

Our interest-based approach is supported by peer-reviewed research demonstrating that interest-matched environments reduce masking, reduce anxiety, and improve social outcomes for Autistic people in ways that generic community settings cannot replicate. Evidence references are available on request and provided to support plan reassessment reports.

How We Work

Delivery and Pricing

Supports at The Nest are delivered alongside other participants who share your interests. This is why you will see multiple team members working together in the space at any one time - each participant has their own support plan, and the environment is designed so that connection happens naturally between people who genuinely have something in common.

Because of how our team operates together, the ratio of support works out to just under 1:1 per participant, despite the shared delivery environment.

Our current pricing:

  • Under 18, weekday: $45 per hour
  • Under 18, weekend: $55 per hour
  • 18 and over: $35 per hour

These rates sit well below the NDIS price limits for community participation and capacity building supports, and reflect our commitment to keeping access affordable. Your support coordinator can confirm which rate applies to your plan and goals.

Spaces are limited. Pricing under shared delivery is calculated differently to individual 1:1 support - your support coordinator can advise on what applies to your plan.

A note on pricing

Our rates are set below NDIS price limits to keep access as broad as possible.

If you are a plan-managed or self-managed participant, your support coordinator or plan manager can confirm how support costs are claimed from your plan.

Coordination - 1:1 Support

Support Coordination and Recovery Coaching

We offer two distinct coordination supports under Registration Code 0106: Support Coordination and Psychosocial Recovery Coaching. Both are funded coordination supports, not coaching programmes. They are delivered one-to-one, are not activity-led, and centre the person, their goals, and what they need to build capacity, connection, and self-direction over time.

Support Coordination helps participants understand and implement their NDIS plans, connect with providers, navigate systems, and build the confidence to direct their own support. It is a coordination function - the support coordinator works alongside you to strengthen your capacity to manage your own life and plan.

Psychosocial Recovery Coaching is specifically for participants living with psychosocial disability. It focuses on building recovery capital, navigating mental health systems, developing daily routines, and working toward a life that feels meaningful and self-directed. Recovery Coaches work alongside participants, not above them. This is a coordination support, not a clinical or therapeutic intervention.

These supports can include:

  • Goal setting and life planning support
  • Managing daily routines and transitions
  • Developing self-advocacy skills
  • Understanding and navigating systems (NDIS, health, education, employment)
  • Building independence in community settings
  • Recovery-focused coordination for participants with psychosocial disability
  • Preparing for and working through life changes

Funding Information

Support Coordination is funded under Registration Code 0106 and is available to participants with Support Coordination in their plan.

Psychosocial Recovery Coaching is funded under its own line item within Registration Code 0106, and is available to participants whose plans include a psychosocial component.

Both are coordination supports. Your Support Coordinator or LAC can advise on which applies to your current plan.

How We Work

How We Handle Fluctuation

Neurodivergent people often experience significant day-to-day variability in capacity, sensory tolerance, executive function, and social availability. We do not treat this as a problem to manage. Our support model is built with this variability as a given, not a deviation.

Sessions can be adjusted, shortened, paused, or shifted in format based on where a participant is on any given day. Progress is not linear, and we do not expect it to be. When something does not work, we look at what needs to change in the environment or approach, not in the person.

Fluctuation is expected. Recalibration is not failure.
Families and Carers

Supporting the People Around You

We recognise that family members and carers often carry a significant load. Where a participant consents, we welcome family involvement in planning, goal setting, and understanding how best to support their person at home and in the community.

We also work collaboratively with families around early childhood supports, where the goals of the participant and the capacity of the family system are closely connected.

A note on boundaries

Our primary relationship is always with the participant. Families and carers are welcome partners in support, but participant authority and decision-making comes first. We work from a position that the participant is the expert on their own experience.

Participant Rights

Your Authority in Your Support

You have the right to direct your support, make choices about your own life, and take risks that are part of living a full and meaningful life. We do not require you to justify your choices to us. Our job is to provide informed, honest, and skilled support that helps you pursue what matters to you.

Dignity of risk means that you have the right to take reasonable risks in pursuit of your goals. Where risk is present, we will have honest conversations, document our discussions, and work with you to make decisions that are genuinely yours. We will not override your choices in the name of safety without a serious and documented reason to do so.

You can change your mind, change your goals, change your support arrangements, or end your engagement with us at any time. We will always work to facilitate a good transition if you choose to move on.
